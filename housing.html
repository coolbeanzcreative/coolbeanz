student housing in waterloo

client project role
Federation of Students Dossier for student advocacy effort regarding housing crisis in Waterloo Graphic Designer

problem

The City of Waterloo has seen an ever-growing population of students as more people are choosing to attend one of Waterloo's three post-secondary institutions. Landlords and property management companies have seen an increase in demand and capitalized on this particularly vulnerable market. Students moving out of their homes for the first time, right our of high school, have no idea about their rights as a tenant and are easily taken advantage of by greedy landlords and properties still unfinished at move-in dates.

solution

In an effort to advocate for the students of Waterloo regarding this housing crisis, the Federation of Students decided to compile news articles and letters as evidence to bring to the Mayor of Waterloo, hoping that it would spark the beginning of change. We created a dossier outlining these articles and letters, while creating an overall brand for future Feds Advocacy documents. We created layouts using greyscale imagery, triangles in Feds yellow to represent the roof of a house, and highlighted imported quotes within the document. In creating this dossier, we were able to communicate the importance of this housing crisis and hope that it will serve as a powerful piece in the conversation between students and government.
